Eating as Ritual Based on SGI Principles

This module formalizes eating as a sacred discipline. Within SGI, the body is treated as Temple, food as offering, and consciousness as priest. Proper nutrition is therefore not only about what is consumed, but how it is prepared, invoked, received, and integrated.

“The Body is the Temple. Food is the Offering. The Mind is the Priest.”

The 3 SGI Pillars Applied to Nutrition

A sacred nutritional framework

Auset — Radical Devotion

Preparation Reverence Stillness

Auset establishes the state of reception. Before food enters the body, the body must be prepared. Space, posture, breath, and attention are part of the meal.

SGI Law: The Temple must be prepared before the offering is received.
  • Clean the eating area
  • Wash the hands
  • Sit down with intention
  • Remove distraction and agitation

Sekert — Words of Power

Invocation Direction Command

Sekert directs the purpose of the meal. In SGI, speech is not decoration. Speech is instruction. The body hears command. The food receives assignment.

SGI Law: What is spoken helps determine what is built.
  • Speak purpose before the first bite
  • Use words of restoration, strength, balance, and clarity
  • Reject careless or chaotic speech during ritual eating

Tehuti — Infinite Wisdom

Selection Discernment Truth

Tehuti governs the choice of substance. Wisdom asks whether a food builds structure, burdens the Temple, or moves the body away from its highest expression.

SGI Law: Only that which aligns with truth should enter the Temple.
  • Prefer whole and living foods
  • Reduce overly processed intake
  • Choose nourishment that supports clarity and longevity

The 2 SGI Principles in Nutritional Practice

Eating as lineage work

Extending Royal Legacy

Every meal should contribute to durability, dignity, and longevity. To eat according to SGI is to eat with future generations in mind. The question is not merely whether a food satisfies now, but whether it preserves royal structure over time.

  • Choose foods that support vitality and endurance
  • Reduce habits that slowly degrade the body
  • View each meal as an investment in lineage

Standing on the Shoulders of Enlightened Ancestors

SGI ritual nutrition honors proven ancestral intelligence. Foods closest to earth, rhythm, season, and natural preparation hold greater harmony than substances overly manipulated by disorder.

  • Favor recognizable food over artificial formulations
  • Honor timing, rhythm, and moderation
  • Eat with memory, gratitude, and continuity

The 5 Possibilities Applied to Eating

An SGI diagnostic lens
1

Aláfíà — Perfect Balance

The meal is balanced, the mind is calm, the setting is ordered, and the body receives nourishment in alignment.

2

Ejìfé — Harmonized Enjoyment

The meal is pleasing, satisfying, and still aligned. Pleasure is present without excess or disorder.

3

Ètàwà — Mixed Condition

Some of the meal supports health while other parts work against it. There is value present, but not purity.

4

Òkànràn — Warning / Imbalance

The meal is rushed, excessive, toxic, emotionally driven, or disconnected from SGI alignment.

5

Òyèkú — Reset / Emptiness

Abstention, fasting, or reduction is used to restore order, reset appetite, and return the Temple to discipline.

The SGI Ritual Sequence

A practical flow for daily use
Phase 1

Prepare

  • Clean the space
  • Wash the hands
  • Sit and breathe
  • Enter stillness
Phase 2

Invoke

  • Speak words of power
  • Declare the purpose of the meal
  • Call in balance, strength, and clarity
Phase 3

Consume with Wisdom

  • Eat slowly
  • Chew with awareness
  • Observe quantity and quality
  • Remain present
Phase 4

Integrate

  • Pause after eating
  • Give thanks
  • Notice body response
  • Close in order, not haste
